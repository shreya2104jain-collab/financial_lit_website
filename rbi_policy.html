RBI Policies

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is the central authority responsible for regulating monetary policy, controlling inflation, and maintaining financial stability in India.

Key RBI Policies

The major policies formulated by the RBI for economic regulation include:

  • Monetary Policy: Setting interest rates, managing inflation, and controlling money supply.
  • Financial Inclusion: Initiatives like PMJDY and financial literacy programs for banking access.
  • Digital Banking: Promoting UPI, online payments, and secure electronic transactions.
  • Banking Regulation: Capital adequacy, liquidity management, and governance rules.
  • Currency Management: Issuing, designing, and circulating secure currency notes and coins.
