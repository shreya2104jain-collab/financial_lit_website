RBI Policies

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is the central bank of India and oversees the monetary policy, manages inflation, and works to maintain financial stability within the country.

Key RBI Policies

The RBI has introduced several important policies that help regulate and stabilize the financial system in India. Some of the major policies include:

  • Monetary Policy: The RBI sets interest rates, determines inflation targets, and manages the money supply to ensure stable economic growth.
  • Financial Inclusion: Initiatives like Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) and financial literacy programs aimed at expanding banking access to all citizens.
  • Digital Banking: Encouraging online banking systems and mobile applications, including the launch of the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) and UPI.
  • Banking Regulation: Ensuring sound practices in the banking sector through policies on capital adequacy, liquidity management, and corporate governance.
  • Currency Management: Issuing and managing the supply of currency notes and coins in the country, and ensuring their integrity and security.
