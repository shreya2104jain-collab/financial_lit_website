Government Policies

The Government of India implements various policies to support economic growth, healthcare, education, and infrastructure. Key policies include initiatives for financial inclusion, tax reforms, subsidies for businesses, and government schemes aimed at welfare and employment generation.

Key Government Policies

The Government of India has introduced a wide range of policies to drive economic development and address societal issues. Some of the most important policies are:

  • Financial Inclusion Policies: Programs aimed at bringing the unbanked population into the formal banking system, such as Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY).
  • Tax Reforms: Introduction of Goods and Services Tax (GST) to simplify the indirect tax structure and ensure uniform tax rates across the country.
  • Make in India: A policy aimed at boosting domestic manufacturing and attracting foreign investment in key sectors.
  • Digital India: A campaign to promote digital literacy and ensure government services are accessible online.
  • Swachh Bharat Abhiyan: A cleanliness drive launched to improve sanitation facilities and eliminate open defecation.
  • Startup India: Policies and incentives to encourage the growth of new businesses and support entrepreneurs.
